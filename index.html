Understanding Fake News

Fake news refers to false or misleading information presented as news. It spreads quickly through social media and online platforms, often mimicking the format of credible journalism.

Types of fake news include:

  • Misinformation: False information shared without harmful intent.
  • Disinformation: Deliberately false information to deceive.
  • Clickbait: Sensational headlines that misrepresent content.
  • Satire/Parody: Humorous but often misunderstood content.

Fake news can undermine democracy, fuel hate speech, mislead the public, and cause real-world harm. It is critical to identify and stop its spread.
